About Us - African Union Development Agency (AUDA-NEPAD)

The New Partnership for Africa’s Development (NEPAD) is the pan-African strategic framework for the socio-economic development of the continent. It is a comprehensive strategic framework for Africa’s renewal and entrenchment of sustainable growth and development within the continent. It is a pledge by African leaders, based on a common vision and a firm and shared conviction to achieve greatness in growth and development in Africa.The Agency is now known as African Union Development Agency (AUDA-NEPAD).

THE AUDA-NEPAD NIGERIAN JOURNEY

NEPAD was adopted in 2001 by the OAU 37th Summit and ratified by the AU in 2002 at its first summit. The NEPAD strategic framework document was prepared by the leaders of the five initiating states (Nigeria, Algeria, Egypt, Senegal and South Africa). African heads of state and government met in Abuja, Nigeria, in October 2001 and adopted the name NEPAD. However following the transformation and domestication of the decision of the AU Heads of State and Government at the 31st Ordinary Session in Nouakchot, Mauritania, 1st – 2nd July 2018 the Agency is now known as African Union Development Agency (AUDA-NEPAD).
